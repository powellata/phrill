Chibok: between a rock and a hard place

Beseiged by Boko Haram and unprotected by the Government what next

Following the recent attack by the Boko Haram on Chibok community which led to the dispersing of the villagers in that community, the Chibok community in Abuja has cried out for help to members of the public to assist their displaced people in setting up a camp in Abuja.

Speaking at the #BringBackOurGirls sit-out in Abuja, the chairman of the Chibok community in Abuja, Tsambido Hosea-Abana said the members of his community, who were displaced by the insurgency were scattered all over the place and the leaders of the community were seeking assistance to erect a temporary camp for them in the Karu axis of the Federal Capital Territory.

Hosea-Abana had revealed that some of the residents of the community remained unaccounted for, but noted that calm had returned to the attacked community.

He frowned at President Barack Obama’s condemnation of Boko Haram’s attack on Chibok community and urged him to take action against the sect members as a world leader to save the situation in the north east.

He also lashed out at the Federal Government for what he described as lack of seriousness in tackling the insurgency and bringing back the remaining 219 Chibok school girls abducted by the Boko Haram group. He contended that peace could be restored in the north east only by divine intervention.

It would be recalled that the Boko Haram sect had attacked and reportedly captured the Chibok community recently before the military assisted by the Civilian Joint Task Force fought back and reclaimed it.

The vice chairman of the Chibok community in Abuja, Mutah Nkeki had raised the alarm of the impending attack on the Chibok community, but nothing was done to protect the town and its people until the group struck. The community, who the insurgents forced out, have become parts of the displaced persons rooming the country as some of their means of livihood have been destroyed by the insurgents.

The Chibok community has been on the spot light in the world after the Boko Haram sect attacked and abducted 276 girls from its Government Secondary School and up until now, the federal government and the security agencies are yet to rescue the remaining 219 students after 57 excaped on their own.
